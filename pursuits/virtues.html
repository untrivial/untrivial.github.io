Franklin's Plan to Achieve Moral Perfection


As of July 2023, I have been following Benjamin Franklin's simple system to improve his character. Franklin drafted a table with 13 columns, each headed by a virtue: Sincerity, Industry, Humility, and so on. Each row represented a day. If he broke a virtue during that day, he would add a mark to the appropriate column. Furthermore, each week Franklin chose a virtue to work on—with exactly 13 virtues, he could uniformly cycle through the 52 weeks of a year.


I've narrowed my list of virtues to six. Below each virtue are Franklin's notes and my humble additions:

  • Sincerity
    • "Use no hurtful deceit; think innocently and justly, and, if you speak, speak accordingly"
  • Moderation
    • "Avoid extremes; forbear resenting injuries so much as you think they deserve."
  • Industry
    • "Lose no time; be always employ’d in something useful; cut off all unnecessary actions."
  • Humility
    • "Imitate Jesus and [Socrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socrates)."
    • "No dogmatical expressions"
    • Don't say "certainly"
    • Point out issues in a roundabout way
  • Cleanliness
    • "Tolerate no uncleanliness in body, clothes, or habitation."
  • Tranquility
    • "Be not disturbed at trifles, or at accidents common or unavoidable."
    • Stop saying trivial things
